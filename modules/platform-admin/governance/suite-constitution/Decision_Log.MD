# 3. Final Decisions

---

## Decision 01 — System Identity

Decision:
Bassan OS is a Workplace Operating System.

Not:
- admin panel
- CRM app
- SaaS dashboard

Impact:
All suites must feel part of one OS.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 02 — User Persona Priority

Decision:
System serves mixed personas.

Primary priority:
Operations & Support Agents.

Impact:
UI must support long operational sessions.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 03 — Density Policy

Decision:
Adaptive density per suite.

High density:
Operational environments.

Medium density:
Dashboards & CRM.

Low density:
Builders & studios.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 04 — Shell Strategy

Decision:
Single system shell across all suites.

Allowed variation:
Minor contextual adjustments only.

Impact:
Suites cannot redefine layout.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 05 — Workspace Priority

Decision:
Workspace is dominant UI area.

Navigation & panels serve workspace.

Impact:
Panels cannot permanently shrink workspace.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 06 — Motion Philosophy

Decision:
Motion used only for orientation.

Forbidden:
Decorative or distracting animations.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 07 — Long-Term Scalability

Decision:
DNA must support:

• dashboards
• inbox systems
• automation builders
• analytics environments
• AI tools

for 3–5 years minimum.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 08 — Evolution Philosophy

Decision:
DNA remains stable.
Features evolve.

Shell remains constant.

Status: Locked

---

## Decision 09 — Governing Principle

Decision:
Consistency before innovation.

Impact:
New features must respect system DNA.

Status: Locked

---

# 4. Enforcement Rule

Future UI changes must not violate:

• Shell strategy
• Density policy
• Workspace dominance
• Navigation behavior

Architecture approval required for exceptions.
